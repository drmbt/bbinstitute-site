DRIVING QUESTION

WHAT HAPPENS WHEN ARTISTS
TAKE OVER A TOWN?
For ten years, the Bombay Beach Biennale
(BBB) has been the answer. BBB is a radically
non-commercial gathering held at the edge of
the Salton Sea, where site-specific art,
experimental philosophy, and collective
hospitality converge.
There are no tickets, no commerce, no
advertising or marketing. The dates are never
published publicly. They’re only spread by
word of mouth. The Bombay Beach Biennale
is not a festival, nor is it a spectator sport. It‘s a
living inquiry into the power art has when it
leaves institutions behind.

THE ORIGIN STORY

THE STORY OF BBB & BBI BEGINS IN BOMBAY BEACH, CALIFORNIA. 3 HOURS DRIVE FROM
LOS ANGELES, BOMBAY BEACH SPANS A HALF-MILE BY A HALF-MILE, HAS APPROXIMATELY
250 FULL-TIME RESIDENTS, AND IS 45 MINUTES AWAY FROM THE NEAREST GROCERY
STORE. IT’S THE SECOND LOWEST PLACE IN THE USA AFTER DEATH VALLEY. IN THE
SUMMER, TEMPERATURES RISE TO 120 DEGREES.
8

BOMBAY BEACH’S PAST

ONCE A THRIVING MID-CENTURY RESORT TOWN,
BOMBAY BEACH WAS BORN DURING THE POSTWAR
REAL ESTATE BOOM. IN THE 1950S AND ’60S, THE
ACCIDENTAL INLAND SEA BECAME CALIFORNIA’S
RIVIERA: A PLAYGROUND OF YACHT CLUBS, MARINAS,
AND LAKESIDE COCKTAIL LOUNGES FREQUENTED BY
CELEBRITIES LIKE FRANK SINATRA & THE BEACH BOYS.

BUT RISING SALINITY & AGRICULTURAL RUNOFF BEGAN
TO CHOKE THE LAKE. FISH POPULATIONS COLLAPSED.
TOURISM EVAPORATED. WHAT FOLLOWED WAS A LONG,
SLOW ECONOMIC DECLINE THAT LEFT BEHIND
SHUTTERED BUILDINGS, EMPTY LOTS, AND A LANDSCAPE
MARKED BY BOTH ABANDONMENT AND ENVIRONMENTAL
CRISIS.

BOMBAY BEACH’S TRANSFORMATION

BBB 2026

WHEN BBB BEGAN IN 2016, 80% OF THE TOWN
WAS ABANDONED. THERE WAS NO VISIBLE ARTS
ACTIVITY, AND FEW VISITORS STAYED OVERNIGHT.
AT BBB’S INVITE, ARTISTS BEGAN REACTIVATING
ABANDONED SPACES THROUGH SITE-SPECIFIC
INSTALLATIONS AND PERFORMANCES, THEREBY
RESHAPING THE TOWN’S CULTURAL NARRATIVE.

WHAT BEGAN AS A LOCAL EXPERIMENT HAS SINCE
GROWN INTO AN INTERNATIONAL ARTS MOVEMENT
DEDICATED TO RADICAL ACCESSIBILITY, PLACEBASED
PRACTICE,
AND
COMMUNITY-ROOTED
CULTURAL PRODUCTION.

BBI’S MISSION

As a steward of Bombay Beach’s evolving identity, the Bombay Beach Institute
facilitates residencies, interdisciplinary conferences, and public programming that
bridge local and global communities. Through art, philosophy, and radical hospitality
alongside the Bombay Beach Biennale, we create a space where artists, thinkers, and
visionaries redefine the possible. In a secluded part of the desert, we create an
alternate reality where radical thought flourishes and theory transforms into praxis
through lived experience and real-world application.
The Institute is a driving force behind the facilitation of community organizing and the
Bombay Beach hivemind, creating a living archive of our resources, innovations, and
programming for our local community and the world at large. Through engagement with
artists and thinkers, The Institute cultivates theoretical frameworks and practical
experiments to reimagine societal structures in a place defined by environmental
catastrophe and socioeconomic collapse.
